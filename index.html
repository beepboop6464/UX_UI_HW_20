Women's Center

Website redesign

A website redesign for a non non-profit organization that helps and provide resources for those escaping or recovering from an abusive situation.

About The Project

The Goal

The overarching goal of this project was to elevate the Women’s Center website, prioritizing user satisfaction through the refinement of its navigation hierarchy. A key objective was to eradicate any potential confusion that users, particularly those experiencing emotional distress, might encounter. Our aim was to facilitate seamless access to essential resources, ensuring an optimal user experience

Phase 1: User Research And Analysis

Research Goals

When conducting our surveys and user interviews our primary goal was to understand the user's needs, particularly in challenging situations like being in an unsafe environments. we wanted our research to lay the groundwork for a redesign that's not only functional but also empathetic and supportive to users in distress, ensuring the interface provides the necessary resources and comfort during difficult times

Competitor Analysis

Our competitor analysis was instrumental in identifying opportunities to differentiate ourselves in the market. Through this analysis, we discovered that many existing options lacked a crucial aspect: discretion. This finding underscores the importance of integrating heightened privacy measures into our solution, ensuring that users feel secure and confident in their interactions

Heuristic Evaluation

In our Heuristic Evaluation, we observed areas for improvement, particularly in the navigation system. The navigation bar appeared cluttered, lacking clear priorities or organization, which could potentially hinder usability, especially in high-pressure situations. Additionally, we noted instances where certain icons resembled links but were non-functional, suggesting a need for adjustments in the visual design to enhance clarity and user experience

Survey

We developed a comprehensive survey to gather invaluable insights from users, aimed at informing our website redesign process. Through thoughtfully crafted questions, we delved into users' expectations, preferences, and pain points. Two notable insights emerged: the optimal navigation structure and users' preferences for calming colors.

Resarch Data Analysis

We analyzed the research data that we had gathered by using the affinity mapping technique, where we thoroughly discussed our individual findings and tried to group them into categories

Phase 2: Proto-Persona Insights

Proto Persona

Uses the data we gathered to craft our proto-persona. We developed a profile of Emily, a 27-year-old married waitress who finds herself in an abusive relationship and seeks a safe way out. This persona encapsulates the challenges faced by individuals in similar situations, highlighting the need for supportive digital solutions. From there, we developed an empathy map to better understand Emily's needs. Key insights include:

  • Users will need to enter and exit the site quickly without being at risk of exposure.
  • Users need quick access to resources; they have little time to navigate the site.
  • Developing effective communication channels between the user and support center.

Story Board

My team developed a storyboard illustrating how someone in a situation similar to our proto-persona, Emily, might interact with the site. Given the sensitive nature of the topic, it's crucial to consider the safety of users who may be accessing the website in the presence of their abuser. To address this concern, the original desktop site already included a quick escape button. However, in our redesign, we recognized the importance of extending this feature to the mobile version to ensure accessibility and safety across all platforms. I took charge of creating the artwork for the storyboard, ensuring that it effectively conveyed Emily's journey and the proposed enhancements to the digital solution.

Problem Statement

Building upon our understanding of the challenges faced by individuals seeking support from the Women’s Center website, we identified a critical issue which we made into our problem statement

  • The Women’s center website was designed to provide those involved in abusive situations with safe resources, support, and aftercare. We have observed that the website has no navigation hierarchy which could cause confusion when looking for fast resources on a time limit. How might we improve the center’s website so clients can quickly and discreetly access resources?

Value Proposition

In response to this problem, our team developed a value proposition centered

  • We believe improving the women’s center website for those needing to escape or heal from an abusive relationship will achieve a safer, more discreet, and overall better experience to recreate a normal life.

Phase 3: Ideation & Brainstorming

Card Sorting and Sitemap

To begin, we structured our sitemap based on the survey data we gathered earlier. Our aim was to ensure that vital information is readily accessible to users. By organizing the site in this manner, we prioritize quick access to key resources and support services

User Task flow

We proceeded by developing a user task flow to gain deeper insights into how users would interact with the site. This helped us identify areas where certain features might need rearrangement or optimization to enhance the user experience

Sketches

In the initial stages of our redesign process, our team began by sketching out ideas on paper prototypes. During this phase, one key decision was to ensure that contact information was prominently featured on the homepage and easily accessible from every page. Additionally, we determined that each page, including the homepage, would include a hero section with a striking image conveying empowerment and reassurance. These sketches laid the foundation for our website's design, allowing us to visualize and refine our ideas before moving into digital prototypes.

Phase 4: Wireframing & Prototyping

low/mid-fidelity prototype

We then translated these insights into wireframes, ensuring our designs were not only user-friendly but also deeply empathetic to the needs of individuals. These wireframes formed the foundation for our initial low-fidelity prototype, reflecting our commitment to crafting a nurturing digital space for those seeking support and empowerment."

User Testing

To refine our designs for the women's centers interface, we conducted user testing with a focus on key functionalities.

  • Assess readability and effectiveness of the safety page.
  • Enable easy access to live chat support.
  • Locate and understand the function of the call feature
  • Verify users' understanding of the exit feature for safety.

By prioritizing these goals we aimed to improve the user experience for individuals navigating sensitive situations

User Testing Key Findings

After conducting user testing, we implemented several minor adjustments to our prototype, such as optimizing the placement of elements and adjusting font sizes. However, the most significant insight gleaned from our testing was the confusion surrounding the exit button represented by an 'x.' Recognizing the potential severity of this issue, particularly in sensitive contexts such as domestic abuse, we opted to enhance clarity by replacing the 'x' with the word 'Escape.' This modification aims to ensure that users intuitively understand the function of the button, thereby improving the overall user experience

Escape feature
